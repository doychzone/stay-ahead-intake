Movement — Functional Baseline Tests
Please perform the following 8 tests before continuing. They take 10-15 minutes and require only a chair, a wall, and a phone timer. There are no good or bad scores — only honest ones.
1
Chair Stand — Strength & Mobility
Stand up from a chair and sit back down without using your arms. Count how many complete stands in 30 seconds.
Reference: 40s: 14-16 · 50s: 12-14 · 60s: 10-12 · 70+: 8-10
2
Single Leg Stand — Balance
Stand on one foot without shoes, hands on hips, eyes open. Time how long you can hold without touching the ground.
Reference: 60+ sec = excellent · 20-60 = good · under 20 = priority area
3
Sit and Reach — Flexibility
Sit on the floor with legs straight. Reach forward toward your toes.
4
Plank Hold — Core & Endurance
Hold a plank position for as long as possible with correct form.
5
Towel Squeeze — Grip Strength
Roll a towel tightly. Squeeze with full force for 5 seconds. Rate your perceived grip strength.
6
Ruler Drop — Reaction Time
Ask someone to hold a ruler vertically at 0 cm. Place your thumb and index finger at the 0 mark. When they drop it without warning — catch it. Repeat 3 times and record the average.
Reference: under 15 cm = excellent · 15-20 = good · over 20 = work needed
7
3-Minute Step Test — Cardiovascular Efficiency
Step up and down on a step (~30 cm) at a rhythm of 24 steps per minute for 3 minutes. Immediately after — measure your heart rate for 1 full minute.
Reference: under 80 BPM = excellent · 80-100 = good · over 100 = priority area
8
Sitting-Rising Test (SRT) — Functional Movement
Sit down on the floor and stand back up without using your hands, knees or elbows for support. Each touch costs one point. Maximum score is 10.
Validated in longevity research as a predictor of all-cause mortality.
